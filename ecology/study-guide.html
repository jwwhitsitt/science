Rule: If you can explain it out loud, you know it. If you can teach it, you OWN it.

Warm-Up: “Which one am I?”

Say the answer first. Then tap to reveal.

1) I make my own food using sunlight.
Producer Reveal
2) I eat plants or animals for energy.
Consumer Reveal
3) I break down dead things so nutrients return to the soil.
Decomposer Reveal
4) I eat only plants.
Herbivore Reveal
5) I eat plants and animals.
Omnivore Reveal
6) I eat only animals.
Carnivore Reveal

Ecosystem Detective

In your own words:
  • Abiotic means…
  • Biotic means…
  • A habitat is…
  • A niche is…
Reveal a good answer set
  • Abiotic: nonliving parts of an environment (water, air, rocks, sunlight)
  • Biotic: living or once-living things (plants, animals, fungi)
  • Habitat: where an organism lives
  • Niche: the organism’s role or “job” in the ecosystem

Food Chains: Tell the story

Make a food chain story out loud using this pattern:
  • Producer → Consumer → Consumer → Decomposer
Example starter: grass → rabbit → fox → fungi

Plants and Fungi (Friendly Battle)

Explain the difference:
  • Plants make food using __________.
  • Fungi get food by __________ things.
Reveal
  • Plants make food using sunlight (photosynthesis).
  • Fungi get food by breaking down (decomposing) things.

Practice Quiz (Quick)

Answer first, then reveal.

1) What is a producer? Give one example.
A producer makes its own food (example: a plant).
2) What’s the difference between biotic and abiotic?
Biotic = living/once living. Abiotic = nonliving.
3) What is a habitat?
Where an organism lives.
4) What is a niche?
The organism’s role/job in the ecosystem.
5) What does a decomposer do?
Breaks down dead things so nutrients go back into the environment.

Final Boss Question

Explain out loud like a scientist:
  • How could you prove a plant needs water, air, and sunlight to grow?
  • What would you keep the same?
  • What would you change?
  • What would you observe?

If you can explain this clearly, you’re test-ready.
